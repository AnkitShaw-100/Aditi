Why capability is not strategy

A short primer on why platforms and weapons only matter when tied to political purpose, doctrine, and sustainment.

Capability is a vocabulary. Strategy is grammar. A military platform can impress on paper and still fail to change outcomes if the state has not answered what the capability is for, how it will be sustained, and what political effect it must create.

For ADITI, the central question is never only what India possesses. It is how the instrument connects to doctrine, initiative, terrain, and integration.
