01The gap

India is becoming a power the world will test. Its strategic thinking can't stay this fragmented.

India's defence conversation is loud but shallow — hardware lists, linear narratives, hot takes that expire in a week. The questions that decide outcomes go unexamined: who controls escalation, what a doctrine costs elsewhere, and where a tactical win quietly becomes a strategic loss.

02The framework — A·D·I·T·I

Most defence media catalogues capability. ADITI interrogates choice.

Every issue runs through the same five lenses — the letters that make the name.

A

Armament

The means of war: weapons, platforms, logistics, industrial capacity, supply chains, and sustainment.
Pillar · A
D

Doctrine

The ideas of war: how force is conceived, organised, and employed to achieve political ends.
Pillar · D
I

Initiative

Agency under uncertainty: who sets the agenda, controls escalation, and dictates tempo.
Pillar · I
T

Terrain

The environment of conflict: physical, political, technological, informational, legal, or cognitive.
Pillar · T
I

Integration

The joining of parts: civil–military fusion, inter-service jointness, whole-of-nation effort.
Pillar · I
